My Story

Thank you so much for checking my website out! I really appreciate it. My name is Nicholas Ho and I'm currently wrapping up my senior year at Arizona State University studying Math + CS (two majors). My goal is to become a world-class researcher like some of my role models [M,Z] [A,G] [R,M] [A,S], developing machine learning models for solving complex problems in healthcare, physics, and biology.

I'm currently applying for graduate programs for the next step in my career. Currently, my research interests lie in finding ways to imbue inductive biases into deep models such as to create physics-informed or biologically-informed models for solving complex problems in biological systems.

I currently work with Professor Abhishek Singharoy for the Structural Systems Biology Lab in ASU for merging statistical mechanics and machine learning to solve problems in biophysics i.e. finding the minimum free energy pathway between two states. I am also currently working with Professor Marinka Zitnik at Harvard Medical School Department of Biomedical Informatics to learn rich latent representations for multimodal models.

Please feel free to reach out to me! I'm always looking for interesting problems to talk about!
