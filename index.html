Welcome!

Hey there! My name is Rohit Nandakumar, and I am a Computational Biology master's student at Carnegie Mellon University. My primary research focus is on ultra-scalable molecular AI, particularly in drug discovery, material science, and green chemistry.

My work involves developing highly scalable deep learning models for molecular property prediction. I am currently working in the Isayev Lab, where I have built a billion-scale SMILES-based GNN for chemical property prediction. This work has applications in drug discovery, battery electrolytes, and space materials.

Previously, I interned at Predictive Oncology, where I worked on high-dimensional Cell Painting data for precision medicine. I also developed an AI-driven multi-omics framework for diagnosing developmental dyslexia at Mayo Clinic.

Check out my GitHub for my latest projects!

Feel free to reach out to me! I'm always interested in discussing research, AI, and scalable computing.
