Thank you for checking out my website! My name is Nicholas Ho and I am a third year Computer Science / Computational Biology PhD student in Carnegie Mellon University. I am incredibly grateful to be advised by Professor Jian Ma and Professor Eric Xing. In the past, I lead the pretraining and evaluation for AIDO.Cell and AIDO.DNA at GenBio.AI. Prior to that, I worked closely with Professor Marinka Zitnik at Harvard Department of Biomedical informatics as a research intern, and Professor Abhishek Singharoy for Reinforcement-learning guided MD simulations.

I care a lot about developing models in biology that genuinely scale with performance and data. In other words, I want to develop true foundation models for biology. I don't think we're there yet, but I believe it soon possible.

Quick Note
Although I would say I work on "foundation models" (which I think is an immensely overloaded term), I believe that the task informs the representation. Although this sounds like it may contradict the point of foundation models, in biology, where the data is scarce, it is important to design the right pretraining objective that is best aligned with the important downstream tasks. By learning the right representation for the right problems with the right inductive biases, then we will have generalizable & scalable models.

Feel free to reach out to me! I'm always looking for interesting problems to talk about!
